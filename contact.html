FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

Who are SparkOn kits for?

They are designed for young learners who are ready to assemble a simple moving robot and observe basic STEM ideas in action.

Can schools or organizations request kits?

Yes. SparkOn is focused on local community spaces, schools, clubs, and youth programs that want approachable STEM activities.

What does support help cover?

Support helps pay for kit materials like printed parts, CR2032 batteries, vibrating motors, instruction cards, and other build supplies.

Is SparkOn a registered nonprofit?

SparkOn is currently a community STEM project, not a registered nonprofit. That is why the site uses “support our mission” instead of calling payments donations.

Are contributions to SparkOn tax deductible?

As of now, no. Contributions to SparkOn are not tax deductible. That may change as SparkOn pursues proper registration within the state of Connecticut.

Where is SparkOn currently serving?

SparkOn is currently focused on Easton, Redding, Monroe, Newtown, Fairfield, Trumbull, and Weston.

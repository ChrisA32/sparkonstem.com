Meet the Bristle Bot prototype: a small moving robot students can assemble and observe in motion.

Bristle Bot Kit

Students snap together a simple moving robot prototype using a printed body, CR2032 battery, and vibrating motor.

Inside the kit

Everything needed for the first build.

Each prototype kit includes the core parts needed to assemble one Bristle Bot and access the online build guide.

01

CR2032 Coin Cell Battery

One battery powers the vibrating motor inside the Bristle Bot.

02

Two 3D-Printed Halves

The two body pieces snap together to form the Bristle Bot shell.

03

Small Vibrating Motor

The motor creates the vibration that makes the robot move.

04

QR Instruction Card

The card links builders to this website for the step-by-step guide.

What students observe

What the build shows.

The Bristle Bot is a simple prototype, but the build introduces real engineering ideas through assembly and observation.

01

Motor vibration

The vibrating motor creates rapid movement that makes the bot shake and come alive.

02

Movement from vibration

Those vibrations create tiny repeated pushes against the surface, which help the bot move.

03

Balance and center of gravity

Students can see how the battery, motor, and body placement affect how steady the bot feels as it moves.

04

Assembly sequence

Following the steps shows why engineering builds often depend on order, alignment, and careful part placement.

Step 01

Gather your parts

Start with the two 3D-printed Bristle Bot halves, one CR2032 coin cell battery, the vibrating motor, and the QR instruction card.

Parts photo coming soonPrinted halves, CR2032 battery, vibrating motor, and instruction card
Step 02

Prep and place the battery

Peel off the white adhesive backing on the battery, then press the battery into the crevice inside one Bristle Bot half.

Battery placement photo coming soonShow the CR2032 battery pressed into the printed half
Step 03

Snap the body together

Line up the two printed halves, then press them together until the Bristle Bot body snaps securely into place.

Snap-fit photo coming soonBoth body halves pressed together
Step 04

Route the motor wires

Thread the motor wires through the two small holes in the Bristle Bot body so the wire ends reach the battery slot.

Wire routing photo coming soonMotor wires entering the battery slot
Step 05

Insert and observe

Slide the CR2032 battery fully into the battery slot, check that the motor turns on, then place the Bristle Bot on a flat surface and watch it move.

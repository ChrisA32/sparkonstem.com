Our Mission

STEM access starts with a spark.

SparkOn helps more students discover STEM through approachable hands-on projects that build curiosity, awareness, and confidence.

Why SparkOn Exists

Curiosity becomes confidence.

Students learn by assembling something real and seeing how each part contributes to motion. SparkOn is built to increase access, awareness, and early interest in STEM through projects students can actually hold.

Founder, Joel Barlow High School

Christopher Alvarado

After moving across three different school districts, I noticed the same pattern in each place: students are curious about building and technology, but meaningful engineering opportunities are not always easy to find.

As a robotics member at Joel Barlow High School, a rising senior, and a student living in Easton, CT, I wanted to create something practical, local, and expandable. SparkOn is my way of helping more students experience engineering through projects they can assemble, understand, and remember.

My goal is to keep expanding SparkOn so more schools, organizations, and communities can give young learners an early spark for STEM.
